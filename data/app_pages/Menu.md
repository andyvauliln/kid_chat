## Nutrition overview (kid ~3.5 years) — practical norms & safety
This is a practical guide (not medical advice). If there are allergies, growth issues, anemia, constipation, or picky eating problems — check with pediatrician.

### Daily structure (simple)
- **3 meals + 2 snacks** is normal.
- Keep meals predictable in timing: **08:00 breakfast**, **13:30 lunch**, **19:00 dinner**, snacks in between.

### What “good” looks like (general)
- **Protein daily** (eggs, fish, chicken, tofu/tempeh, beans) to support growth.
- **Vegetables daily** (small portions count; rotate colors).
- **Fruit daily** (banana + other fruit options).
- **Water** frequently. Limit juice.

### Portions (easy approach)
- Use the **child’s hand** as a guide:
  - **Protein**: ~palm size portion per meal (can be smaller if he eats multiple protein sources).
  - **Carbs (rice/potato/bread/oats)**: ~fist size per meal.
  - **Vegetables**: start small (2–3 tbsp) and build up.
  - **Fruit**: small fruit/half fruit per snack, 1–2 snacks/day.

### Key nutrients to cover (where to get them)
- **Iron** (important): beef, chicken, fish, egg yolk, beans + pair with **vitamin C** (orange/guava/tomato) to absorb better.
- **Calcium + vitamin D**: milk/cheese/tofu (calcium-set), small fish with soft bones; sunlight helps vitamin D.
- **Omega‑3**: fish (especially oily fish).
- **Fiber**: fruits, vegetables, oats; helps constipation.

### What is not recommended / risky
- **High salt** foods often reduce appetite for real meals and are not good for blood pressure long-term.
- **High sugar** → mood swings, tooth decay, poor appetite.
- **Choking hazards**: cut grapes, cook crunchy vegetables, crush/chop nuts (or use nut butter thinly spread).
- **Fish bones**: always check carefully (use boneless fillet when possible).

## Food options — nutrients, benefits, concerns (quick notes)
- **Eggs (boiled)**:
  - **Nutrients**: protein, choline, vitamin A, vitamin D, B12, selenium.
  - **Benefits**: growth, brain support, easy and cheap.
  - **Concerns**: allergy risk (if any signs, stop and consult); too much fried/oily prep is not ideal.
- **Bananas**:
  - **Nutrients**: potassium, vitamin B6, carbs, some fiber.
  - **Benefits**: easy snack, energy, often helps appetite.
  - **Concerns**: can worsen constipation in some kids (balance with water + other fruit/fiber).
- **Snake fruit (salak)**:
  - **Nutrients**: fiber, some vitamin C, antioxidants (varies).
  - **Benefits**: snack variety, fiber.
  - **Concerns**: can be constipating for some kids; watch stool + water intake.
- **Porridge / oats + nuts (nuts must be prepared safely)**:
  - **Nutrients**: carbs + fiber; nuts add healthy fats, magnesium, vitamin E.
  - **Benefits**: steady energy, digestion support.
  - **Concerns**: whole nuts = choking hazard; use **crushed nuts** or thin nut butter; watch allergies.
- **Fish (boneless, cooked)**:
  - **Nutrients**: protein, iodine, selenium; omega‑3 (depends on fish type).
  - **Benefits**: brain development support, high-quality protein.
  - **Concerns**: bones; avoid very salty/fried fish often.
- **Chicken / beef / tofu / tempeh / beans**:
  - **Nutrients**: protein; beef/beans add iron; tofu/tempeh add calcium (sometimes) + magnesium.
  - **Benefits**: growth, iron support.
  - **Concerns**: processed meatballs/nuggets can be salty; prefer simple home prep.
- **Vegetable soup / stir-fry veggies**:
  - **Nutrients**: fiber, vitamins A/C/K, minerals.
  - **Benefits**: immunity support, digestion.
  - **Concerns**: too spicy or too oily reduces acceptance—keep mild. Consider mixing finely chopped or pureed vegetables into his rice and chicken.
